Due Diligence in Retail Lending - A Practical Guide

WHY THIS " PRACTICAL GUIDE" ?

Retail lending is a very important segment of banks' advances portfolio and its importance has been growing over the years.

In Retail lending , the number of fresh proposals to be handled at the branch is large and the customer expects the quickest possible turnaround time (TAT). This calls for thorough job knowledge about the bank's retail loan schemes along with special skills of borrower appraisal on the part of loan processing officer and the Branch Manager concerned. Further, although the average per account exposure is relatively small, the number of accounts at any bank branch is typically large which necessitates investing considerable time and effort for maintaining quality of this portfolio.

The controlling authorities in the banks have been issuing circulars and instructions from time to time on "What to do", Why to do", "When to do", Who should do" etc. in respect of Retail lending but seldom clarify "How to do"? Hence, in practice Officers handling Retail credit at branches as well as in the administrative offices, interpret such instructions in their own way and may miss out some of them. At times, this results in two officers / authorities approaching the same issue in different manner or even the same officer taking divergent views on the same issue on different occasions.

This 'Practical guide' is meant precisely to ensure clarity and uniformity in the approach in handling Retail credit by officers concerned at all levels.

An officer missing out on issues having direct impact on the quality of the asset and recovery of the loan, may result in financial loss to the bank and mental stress to the concerned officer. Even if the number of such cases may not be too large, this certainly has a de-motivating impact on the officers handling credit function at different levels. Consequently, many among the new generation of young bankers may lose confidence leading to drop in their efficiency and effectiveness and shying away from dispensing retail credit.

We, the authors, with our hands on experience of over thirty five years each in practical banking as well as teaching, spotted and recognised this reality in the system. We have therefore brought out this 'Practical guide' comprising comprehensive check lists to cover the entire gamut of credit dispensation in Retail lending. This will facilitate their handling of Retail credit in a systematic manner without missing out any relevant issue.
